# Keyword Research & Content Strategy Guide
## Updated: March 2026

---

## Keyword Research Workflow

### Step 1: Seed Keyword Discovery

Start with what the site actually covers, then expand:

1. **Core topics**: what does the site/business primarily help people with?
2. **Audience language**: how does the target audience phrase their questions?
3. **Competitor vocabulary**: what keywords do top-ranking competitors target?
4. **Question mining**: mine "People Also Ask" boxes, Reddit threads, Quora, and community forums
5. **Semantic expansion**: related terms, synonyms, abbreviations ("PLG" and "product-led growth")

### Step 2: Intent Classification

Every keyword has a dominant intent — optimize for it or you'll fail to rank even with great content.

| Intent | Pattern | What User Wants |
|---|---|---|
| **Informational** | "what is X", "how to X", "X explained", "X guide" | Learn, understand |
| **Navigational** | "[Brand] X", "[tool] login" | Find a specific site/page |
| **Commercial** | "best X", "X vs Y", "X alternatives", "X review" | Research before deciding |
| **Transactional** | "buy X", "X pricing", "X free trial", "download X" | Take action/purchase |

### Step 3: Difficulty Assessment (Without Paid Tools)

Assess difficulty by examining who currently ranks:

| Signal | Low Difficulty | High Difficulty |
|---|---|---|
| Domain Authority of ranking sites | Low DA sites rank (DA 20-40) | Only high DA sites rank (DA 60+) |
| Content depth of top results | Shallow, generic pages | Comprehensive, authoritative guides |
| Age of top results | Recent articles rank | Content from 2018-2020 dominates |
| Commercial intent | Mostly informational | Heavily commercialized, paid ads |
| Brand presence | Independent sites rank | Huge brands dominate (HubSpot, Forbes, Investopedia) |

**Opportunity sweet spot**: keywords where you have topical expertise, competition hasn't produced truly comprehensive content, and the intent matches content you can actually create.

### Step 4: Keyword Prioritization Matrix

| Priority | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **P1** | High search intent match + medium difficulty + clear content format |
| **P2** | High volume + feasible difficulty + builds topic authority |
| **P3** | Long-tail, low competition, high commercial intent |
| **Hold** | High volume but extremely high difficulty (HubSpot, Forbes, Wikipedia dominate) |

## Content Gap Analysis

### Funnel Stage Mapping

Every site should have content across all three stages:

| Stage | Query Patterns | Gap Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU (Awareness)** | "what is X", "X definition", "X meaning", "what does X mean" | Foundation — no TOFU means no top-of-funnel discovery |
| **MOFU (Consideration)** | "how to X", "X guide", "best way to X", "X framework", "X strategy" | Main conversion driver for content sites |
| **BOFU (Decision)** | "X vs Y", "X template", "X examples", "X checklist", "X tools" | High-intent, high-conversion; often fastest to rank |

**Common gap pattern**: many content sites are strong MOFU (how-to guides) but missing TOFU (definitional pages) and BOFU (templates and comparisons). Adding TOFU anchors the entire topic cluster.

### Content Gap Detection Process

1. List your target keyword clusters
2. Check current rankings for each cluster's head term
3. Identify clusters where you have no ranking page
4. Prioritize: highest-volume unranked terms first
5. Create content plan: one pillar per cluster + 6-12 supporting posts

## Pillar Page Strategy

### What is a Pillar Page?
A comprehensive, authoritative guide targeting a high-volume head keyword. It's the "anchor" page for an entire topic cluster.

### Pillar Page Characteristics
- **Length**: 3,000–5,000 words (comprehensive coverage of the topic)
- **Breadth**: covers every subtopic of the head term
- **Internal links**: links to all cluster content on related subtopics
- **Format**: long-form guide, organized with clear H2/H3 structure
- **Schema**: Article or WebPage schema with FAQ section

### Topic Cluster Structure
```
Pillar Page: "What Is Product-Led Growth? (Complete Guide)"
  ↓ Links to + receives links from:
  ├── "PLG vs Sales-Led Growth"
  ├── "Product-Led Growth Examples"
  ├── "PLG Metrics to Track"
  ├── "How to Implement PLG"
  ├── "PLG Pricing Strategies"
  └── "Product-Led Growth Companies"
```

### Internal Linking Rules
- Every cluster post links back to the pillar (with descriptive anchor text)
- Pillar links to every cluster post
- Cluster posts link to each other where topically relevant
- No orphan pages — every post has ≥ 2 internal links pointing to it

## Industry-Specific Keyword Strategies

### SaaS / B2B Software

**Primary clusters:**
- Product category keywords: "what is [category]", "[category] software"
- Use case keywords: "[software] for [use case]"
- Comparison keywords: "[your tool] vs [competitor]"
- Integration keywords: "[your tool] + [integration]"
- Job-to-be-done keywords: "how to [accomplish task with your tool]"

**Pillar types:**
- Category definition page ("What is [software category]?")
- Comparison page ("[Category] Software: Top Tools Compared")
- Use case guide ("How to [key use case] with [category]")

### E-commerce

**Primary clusters:**
- Product category pages (high-volume, commercial)
- Best-of lists ("Best [product type] for [use case]")
- Comparison pages ("[Product A] vs [Product B]")
- Gift guides and seasonal content
- How-to / care instructions (long-tail, builds authority)

### Local Business

**Primary clusters:**
- "[Service] in [City]" — primary driver
- "[Service] near me" — high-intent local
- "[City] [Business Type]" — navigational
- "[Service] cost / pricing" — BOFU
- "[Service] reviews" — trust-building

### Publisher / Content Site

**Primary clusters:**
- Definitional: "what is [core topic]"
- How-to: "how to [do main thing]"
- Best practices: "X best practices" / "top X strategies"
- Templates and frameworks: "[topic] template", "[topic] framework"
- Case studies: "[topic] examples", "real-world [topic]"

## Content Calendar Framework

### Cadence by Business Type

| Business Type | Recommended Cadence |
|---|---|
| SaaS / Software | 2-4 posts/month (quality > quantity) |
| E-commerce | Seasonal + evergreen mix; 4-8/month |
| Local Business | 2-4/month + local event coverage |
| Publisher | 4-8/month (high quality essays) or daily (news/tips) |
| Personal Site / Creator | 2-4/month; consistency > frequency |

### Content Mix (% allocation)

| Content Type | Allocation |
|---|---|
| TOFU definitional / pillar pages | 20% |
| MOFU how-to / guide content | 40% |
| BOFU comparison / template content | 20% |
| Link-worthy assets (research, tools, data) | 10% |
| GEO-optimized FAQ / answer blocks | 10% |

## Competitor Keyword Gap Analysis

### Process

1. Identify 3-5 direct competitors
2. For each competitor, identify their top-ranking content (use site:competitor.com in Google, then check keywords)
3. Map competitor keyword coverage against your own
4. Identify: keywords they rank for that you don't
5. Prioritize: topics where competitor content is shallow (opportunity to produce better content)

### Keyword Opportunity Score Formula

Quantitative prioritization for content calendar decisions:

**Formula**: `Opportunity = (Volume × Intent Value) / Difficulty`

**Intent Value weights**:

| Search Intent | Value Multiplier | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | 1× | High volume, low conversion |
| Navigational | 1× | Brand-specific, limited opportunity |
| Commercial | 2× | Research phase, high conversion potential |
| Transactional | 3× | Highest conversion rate |

**Opportunity Matrix** (for quick assessment):

| Scenario | Volume | Difficulty | Intent | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick Win** | Low–Med | Low | Commercial/Transactional | Highest — do first |
| **Growth** | High | Medium | Commercial | High — invest in quality |
| **Long-term** | High | High | Transactional | Medium — requires authority |
| **Research** | Low | Low | Informational | Low — good for topic clusters |

**Priority Scoring** (weighted): Score each keyword 1–5 on these factors, then calculate `Σ(Factor Weight × Score) / 5`:

| Factor | Weight |
|---|---|
| Business Relevance | 30% |
| Keyword Difficulty (inverse) | 25% |
| Search Volume | 20% |
| Search Intent Match | 15% |
| Trend Direction | 10% |

| Priority | Score | Action |
|---|---|---|
| P0 — Must Target | 4.0–5.0 | Create content immediately |
| P1 — High Value | 3.0–3.9 | Queue for next content sprint |
| P2 — Opportunity | 2.0–2.9 | Plan for future calendar |
| P3 — Monitor | 1.0–1.9 | Track but don't prioritize |

### Competitive Advantage Framework

| Advantage | How to Leverage |
|---|---|
| **Deeper expertise** | Write more comprehensive pillar pages |
| **First-hand experience** | Include case studies, original data, unique insights |
| **Specific niche** | Target long-tail keywords big competitors ignore |
| **Speed of publishing** | Capture trending topics before competition |
| **Content format** | Add templates, tools, calculators competitors lack |
| **Original research** | Publish surveys/studies that become citation magnets |
