## Local SEO Overview

Local SEO targets searches with geographic intent — "plumber near me", "best pizza in Austin", "dentist open Sunday". The primary battleground is the **Local Pack** (the 3-map-result block) and **Google Business Profile (GBP)** visibility, supplemented by traditional organic rankings.

**Local vs. Traditional SEO:**
| Factor | Local SEO | Traditional SEO |
|---|---|---|
| Primary signal | Proximity + GBP | Authority + content |
| SERP feature | Local Pack (map 3-pack) | Blue links + featured snippets |
| Key ranking factors | GBP completeness, reviews, citations | Backlinks, content, E-E-A-T |
| Schema priority | LocalBusiness + GeoCoordinates | Varies by type |

## Google Business Profile (GBP)

GBP is the single highest-impact local SEO asset. A fully optimized GBP can outrank a site with far more traditional authority in the Local Pack.

### GBP Completeness Checklist

**Basic Information**
- [ ] Business name matches real-world signage exactly (no keyword stuffing)
- [ ] Primary category is the most specific applicable category
- [ ] Secondary categories added (up to 10 — use all relevant ones)
- [ ] Address: complete, formatted correctly, matches website and citations
- [ ] Phone number: local area code preferred over toll-free for local signals
- [ ] Website URL: link to most relevant page (can be a location-specific landing page)
- [ ] Business hours: complete including holidays (use "special hours" feature)
- [ ] Service area: set if you serve customers at their location (not storefront-only)

**GBP Content**
- [ ] Business description: 750-character description with primary keywords in first 250 characters
- [ ] Services: all services listed with names and descriptions
- [ ] Products: listed with photos and prices if applicable
- [ ] Attributes: all applicable attributes selected (Black-owned, Women-led, Outdoor seating, etc.)
- [ ] Q&A: seed with 3-5 common questions and answer them yourself
- [ ] Posts: weekly or bi-weekly GBP posts (offers, events, updates, products)

**Media**
- [ ] Cover photo: high-quality, 1024×576px minimum, represents the business clearly
- [ ] Logo: square format, high-quality
- [ ] Photos: minimum 10 interior + exterior photos; update regularly
- [ ] Videos: 30-second business overview video (optional but impactful)
- [ ] Street View: ensure Google Maps shows correct entry point

### GBP Posts Best Practices

| Post Type | Use For | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Offer** | Time-limited promotions, discounts | When running promotions |
| **What's New** | General updates, announcements | Weekly |
| **Event** | Scheduled events | Per event |
| **Product** | Feature a specific product or service | Monthly per product |

Post guidelines:
- Include a CTA button (Book, Order, Learn More, Call)
- Posts expire after 7 days (offers expire on set date) — post consistently
- Include a photo in every post (1200×900px recommended)
- First 80 characters are visible before "more" truncation — lead with value

### GBP Ranking Factors

| Factor | Weight | How to Optimize |
|---|---|---|
| **Relevance** | High | Category selection, business description, services listed |
| **Distance** | High | Cannot change — proximity to searcher; service area helps |
| **Prominence** | High | Reviews, backlinks, citations, brand mentions |
| **GBP completeness** | Medium | Fill all fields; complete businesses rank better |
| **Review velocity** | Medium | Consistent new reviews (not a sudden spike) |
| **Review sentiment** | Medium | High star rating + keyword-rich review text |
| **GBP posts** | Low-Medium | Active profiles rank better than inactive |

## Review Strategy

Reviews are the #1 trust signal for local businesses and a top Local Pack ranking factor.

### Review Acquisition

**Timing is everything:**
- Request review at the moment of highest satisfaction (immediately after service completion)
- Text message request > email > in-person ask for response rate
- Make it easy: send a direct link to your GBP review page

**Direct GBP review link:**
```
https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=[PLACE_ID]
```
Find your Place ID: Go to your GBP dashboard → View Profile → Get your review link

**Review request message template:**
```
Hi [Name], thank you for choosing [Business]! We'd love to hear about your experience. 
If you have 2 minutes, leaving us a review would mean the world to us: [link]
```

**Review platforms by industry:**
| Industry | Priority Platforms |
|---|---|
| Restaurants | Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor |
| Healthcare | Google, Healthgrades, Zocdoc |
| Legal | Google, Avvo, Justia |
| Home services | Google, Yelp, HomeAdvisor, Angi |
| B2B SaaS | Google, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot |
| E-commerce | Google, Trustpilot, site reviews |

### Review Response Protocol

**Always respond to ALL reviews — positive and negative:**

| Review Type | Response Goal | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| 5-star | Thank + include business name + keyword | Warm, brief |
| 4-star | Thank + address any concern | Friendly |
| 3-star | Acknowledge + invite to resolve | Empathetic |
| 1-2 star | Acknowledge concern + offer offline resolution | Professional, calm |

**Never:**
- Argue with a reviewer publicly
- Offer incentives for changing a review (Google TOS violation)
- Post fake reviews (a de-ranking risk)
- Ignore negative reviews

### Review Benchmarks

| Metric | Good | Needs Work |
|---|---|---|
| Star rating | ≥ 4.3 | < 4.0 |
| Total reviews | 50+ | < 20 |
| Review recency | New review within 30 days | Last review > 90 days ago |
| Response rate | > 80% | < 50% |

## NAP Consistency (Name, Address, Phone)

**NAP** must be 100% consistent across all online sources. Inconsistencies confuse Google and reduce local ranking trust.

### NAP Format Rules

Define a canonical NAP and use it everywhere:
```
Business Name: Joe's Plumbing LLC          ← Exact legal or trading name
Address: 123 Main Street, Suite 4          ← Full street + suite/unit
City, State ZIP: Austin, TX 78701          ← Abbreviated state, 5-digit ZIP
Phone: (512) 555-0123                      ← Local area code, consistent format
```

**Common inconsistencies to audit:**
- "Street" vs. "St." vs. "St"
- "Suite" vs. "Ste" vs. "#4"
- "(512) 555-0123" vs. "512.555.0123" vs. "5125550123"
- Business name with vs. without "LLC", "Inc.", "&" vs. "and"

### NAP Audit Process

1. Search brand name in Google → check all listings in results
2. Check major aggregators: Yext, Moz Local, BrightLocal — they show citation status
3. Check key directories: Yelp, Yellow Pages, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook, LinkedIn
4. For each inconsistency: claim the listing and correct it

### Citation Building Priority Order

**Tier 1 — Critical (do first):**
- Google Business Profile
- Apple Maps (Apple Business Connect)
- Bing Places for Business
- Yelp (claim existing or create new)
- Facebook Business Page

**Tier 2 — High Value:**
- Better Business Bureau (BBB)
- Chamber of Commerce
- Yellow Pages / YP.com
- Foursquare
- Mapquest
- Superpages

**Tier 3 — Industry-Specific:**
- HomeAdvisor / Angi (home services)
- Healthgrades / ZocDoc (healthcare)
- Avvo (legal)
- TripAdvisor (restaurants/hospitality)
- G2 / Capterra (SaaS/software)
- Houzz (interior design/home)

**Tier 4 — Local/Regional:**
- Local newspaper or community site listings
- City-specific business directories
- Industry association directories (NARI, AIA, ABA, etc.)

## Local Landing Pages

For businesses serving multiple locations or service areas.

### When to Create Location Pages

- Physical storefronts in multiple cities/neighborhoods → Yes, one page per location
- Service area business (SAB) serving 5+ cities → Yes, one page per city
- Service area business serving 2-3 cities → Optional; one page may be sufficient
- Single location → GBP + homepage is usually sufficient

### Location Page Requirements

Each page must have **genuinely unique content** (not 90%+ duplicated):
- [ ] City/location name in title, H1, first paragraph
- [ ] Unique description of services in that location
- [ ] Local landmarks or community references (authentic, not forced)
- [ ] Location-specific photos (not stock photos)
- [ ] Unique phone number (or call tracking per location)
- [ ] Embedded Google Map for that location
- [ ] Location-specific customer testimonials
- [ ] LocalBusiness schema with location-specific address/phone
- [ ] Internal links from homepage and service pages

**Thin location pages (< 300 unique words, > 80% duplicated across locations)** are a Helpful Content risk. Either add genuine value or use a single consolidated page with service area mentions.

## Local Schema Markup

### LocalBusiness Schema Template

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Joe's Plumbing LLC",
  "image": "https://site.com/logo.jpg",
  "url": "https://site.com",
  "telephone": "(512) 555-0123",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "123 Main Street, Suite 4",
    "addressLocality": "Austin",
    "addressRegion": "TX",
    "postalCode": "78701",
    "addressCountry": "US"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": 30.2672,
    "longitude": -97.7431
  },
  "openingHoursSpecification": [
    {
      "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
      "dayOfWeek": ["Monday", "Tuesday", "Wednesday", "Thursday", "Friday"],
      "opens": "08:00",
      "closes": "18:00"
    }
  ],
  "priceRange": "$$",
  "aggregateRating": {
    "@type": "AggregateRating",
    "ratingValue": "4.8",
    "reviewCount": "127"
  }
}
```

**Use the most specific `@type` available:**
- `Plumber`, `Dentist`, `Restaurant`, `LegalService`, `AutoRepair` → more specific than generic `LocalBusiness`
- Full list: [schema.org/LocalBusiness](https://schema.org/LocalBusiness) → see subtypes

## AI Search & Local SEO

AI Overviews and local recommendations are an emerging battleground.

### Local GEO Signals (2026)

- **Review rich content**: AI systems extract review sentiment — businesses with keyword-rich, detailed reviews get cited more
- **Q&A on GBP**: Frequently-asked questions on GBP show up in AI answer synthesis
- **Structured location data**: GeoCoordinates schema helps AI systems understand location relevance
- **Local link signals**: Links from local news, chambers, and community sites signal local authority to AI
- **Reddit mentions**: r/[city] discussions about local businesses increasingly sourced by AI

## Local SEO Scoring

| Dimension | Excellent | Good | Needs Work |
|---|---|---|---|
| GBP completeness | All fields, posts weekly | Most fields, occasional posts | Sparse or unclaimed |
| Star rating | ≥ 4.5 | 4.0-4.4 | < 4.0 |
| Review count | 100+ | 20-99 | < 20 |
| NAP consistency | 100% across all Tier 1-2 | Mostly consistent | Inconsistencies present |
| Citation count | 50+ relevant citations | 20-49 | < 20 |
| Local schema | Complete, validates, specific type | Present but generic | Missing |
| Location pages | Unique content, local schema | Thin but present | Duplicate or missing |
